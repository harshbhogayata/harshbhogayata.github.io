Getting Started in Cybersecurity: A Student's Perspective

Oct 24, 2024 #network-security 5 min read

Cybersecurity is often portrayed in movies as fast-paced typing on green screens (which, ironically, I've adopted for this portfolio). However, the reality is a mix of deep technical knowledge, persistent problem-solving, and continuous learning.

1. The Foundation

Before you can break it, you must know how to build it. My journey started with understanding:

  • Networking: TCP/IP, OSI Model, DNS, HTTP.
  • Linux: Command line proficiency is non-negotiable.
  • Programming: Python for automation, Bash for scripting.

2. Certifications

While hands-on skills are king, certifications help structure your learning. I recommend:

  • CompTIA Security+: The gold standard for entry-level theory.
  • Google Cybersecurity Certificate: Great for practical labs and Python exposure.
  • eJPT / OSCP: For those leaning towards the Red Team (Offensive Security).

3. Practice, Practice, Practice

You can't learn swimming by reading a book. Use platforms like:

  • TryHackMe: Guided learning paths.
  • HackTheBox: Real-world CTF challenges.
  • OverTheWire: Wargames to learn Linux security.

Conclusion

The field is vast. Don't try to learn everything at once. Pick a niche (Web, Network, Forensics), go deep, and then expand. Stay curious!
